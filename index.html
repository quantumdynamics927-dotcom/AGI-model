Current judgment

  • Architecturally excellent Calibration is now a first-class layer in the promoter pipeline.
  • Methodologically stronger The repository now models systematic hardware effects rather than treating them as unexplained misses.

What is scientifically good here

  • The offset-only model is a reasonable v1 because the observed error appears systematic, not chaotic.
  • The backend appears stable while promoter-specific spread is compressed into a much narrower measured range, which is informative for future design.
  • The repository already supports this direction through IBM execution, promoter panels, and artifact governance.

What to avoid overclaiming

  • Do not present the 100% post-calibration pass rate as the central scientific claim.
  • Do not treat the current five-job batch as proof of calibration portability.
  • Keep the baseline near 1/φ as an observational note until it reproduces across more jobs and backends.
